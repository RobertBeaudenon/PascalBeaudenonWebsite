About Pascal Beaudenon

From War Reporter to Landscape Photographer

Biography

Pascal Beaudenon was born on September 24, 1965, in a small village in Southwest France called Aire sur l'Adour. Very early on, his family moved farther south to the Basque country, but the Beaudenons would soon start moving from one country to another throughout the world.

While traveling extensively from a very young age, Pascal got "bitten by the photography bug." One day in 1982, he discovered the images of war-torn Vietnam, taken a decade earlier by photographer Tim Page. His decision was already made‚Äîhe would not stop thinking of his future profession as a photo reporter.

This ambition began to be fulfilled in April 1989 when he arrived in Beirut, his first stop. He would then work as an independent reporter from ex-Yugoslavia to the Caucasus, until the eve of the second Iraqi conflict.

Residing in Lebanon since 1994 and now a family man, Pascal decided in 2002 to devote himself to his work in a different way. Still on his mind were the memories of his earliest years lived under the sun of Africa and the South Pacific:

"I had been lucky to live in places surrounded by gorgeous landscapes during all these years. This is merely a return to my roots..."

Pascal Beaudenon has spent years focusing on the precious beauty and richness of nature in Lebanon, without resorting to any artifice. The beauty of the panoramas he offers us is the same as we would have experienced first-hand at the photographed sites.
